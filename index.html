How I coach

The ETHIC Method.

Breakthroughs aren't luck. They come from the right environment. ETHIC is the five things I make sure are true in every conversation. They're the difference between feeling good for an hour and changing what you do on Monday.

E

Empowered

You leave feeling confident and in control of your transition, not reactive to circumstances. The point is movement, not reflection for its own sake.

T

Taught

You gain frameworks for thinking intentionally that stick with you long after our work ends. Practical tools, not theory.

H

Heard

Your specific situation, values, and aspirations get fully understood and honored. Most high-performers don't have anyone who really listens without an agenda.

I

Inspired

You move past limiting beliefs and connect to a bigger vision of what's possible. Not hype. Genuine possibility you hadn't considered.

C

Challenged

I push you past safe thinking and name the limiting beliefs you've stopped questioning. You're hiring me to help you think bigger, not to be agreed with.

I ask more questions than I give answers. When you say it out loud yourself, it sticks.

I've been doing this for years. I just didn't call it coaching.

For most of my career, I was the person colleagues, founders, and friends came to when they were stuck at a real crossroads. A job decision, a business that had stopped working the way they built it, the question of whether to leap. I'd help them cut through the noise and find the move that was actually theirs to make.

Eventually I realized that that was the work. Not the title on the org chart. It was the part where I help someone remove the belief that's holding them in place and see the bigger version of what's possible.

My approach is direct and practical. No vision boards. No "find your passion" sessions. Just honest conversation, frameworks that work in the real world, and someone in your corner who'll tell you the truth and hold you to the plan.

I run a sales consulting practice too, which keeps me close to how real businesses actually grow. But this, coaching people through what's next, is the work I believe I was put here to do.
